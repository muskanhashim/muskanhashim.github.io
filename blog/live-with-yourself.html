learning to live with your (sins) self

july 2022

in the real world, no one claps when you do the right thing.

you feel like the better person for a moment until you’re yanked back to reality. a reality where the one good thing you did does not make you a good person. nothing can make you a good person because there is no such thing as good people. good things are things you ought to do normally, but don’t, because you’re not obligated to. that’s why they only make you feel good for a moment because you’re supposed to do them more often. in a world that normalizes and encourages psychopathic traits, you’re part of the problem because you think your good actions could compensate.

the way we atone for our sins is by having to live with ourselves.

good actions are nothing but escapes from ourselves. they are false hopes for a couple of minutes that allow us to believe we could be better. they don’t last.

i believe in that; everyone is shrewd, manipulative, and fiendish. because we grow up in a world that normalizes, even encourages, psychopathic traits. you grow up starry-eyed looking down on people, thinking you’re better than them. one day you will realize that you cannot impose exceptionally high, or even completely normal, standards on other people and yourself.

we all try to do better but at the end of the day, we all fail.

tldr; good actions do nothing to compensate for bad actions. those who do terrible things have to live with themselves, and that’s how we atone for our sins.

hurtful actions can only be forgiven, not forgotten. people don’t let you down, your expectations of them do.
