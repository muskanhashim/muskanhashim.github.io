On moving through life, with intention

July 4, 2026

I would like to invite you to partake in an interesting experiment. For one day, and it is somewhat important if it starts in the morning, or whenever you prefer to begin your day:

As you move through your routine, or lack thereof, make note of how many things you do, if any, that are “borrowed”.

For example, I started adding a dash of cinnamon to my coffee for some time, because a friend spoke about it with such a gleam in their eyes. Now the cinnamon jar at my home sits next to the coffee machine.

I used to wage wars against all non-stick pans, with metal forks and wired scrubs, every time I cooked. I used to rush through my day in an uncalled-for hurry, as if I was always running late for something, even when I wasn’t. I would stomp my feet on the ground instead of placing them down with thoughtful intention. I used to bask in the pride of having accomplished many things in little time, by multitasking. I used to slouch as a teenager, not because I didn’t have proper guidance on posture, but because I was scared to grow into the body of a woman.

I try not to do those things anymore, unless the situation calls for it.

How much of our behaviors are trauma responses, and what are we if we allow ourselves to not let our life experiences shape the way we act, even at micro scales?

Not healed, but a step closer to living in the present without letting our past define us, unless where we choose to, of course. Allowing ourselves to select, and not be run by, our quirks (quark).

The need to rush through things despite having enough time, and forcing things instead of guiding them into place, were once a way for me to signal to the world that I had been through times where I juggled many demanding roles at once, attempting to perfectly balance it all. I don’t need to do that anymore. No one should have to do that. And so I no longer need to carry it in the way I move through life. It was a phase that shaped me, yes, but does not predestine or define my trajectory.
