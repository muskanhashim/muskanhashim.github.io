i'm nobody's daughter

september 2020

sometimes, when it feels as if the weight of the world is crushing me down and after bearing it without saying a word for weeks and months, when i feel i can’t anymore, i remind myself i’m nobody’s daughter.

nobody is different from a normal parent. nobody will tell me it’ll be okay, and nobody will assure me that they have my back in case things go south, in case anything bad happens.

nobody has got my back. nobody will put a loving hand over my head, stroke my hair, and tell me that i’m protected. and nobody will try to make me laugh by pretending to threaten whoever’s bothering me.

nobody asks me if i’ve eaten or where i live or if i have a roof over my head where i feel like i belong. nobody will tell me that even if i end up making a series of bad decisions, or for once let loose and act on my feelings like people my age, i would still have a home somewhere. and food. nobody will tell me i’m allowed to have emotions or feel tired. it is nobody’s place.

you see, nobody took the place of somebody who thought his daughter was nobody. and ever since then, i’ve always had nobody.
