on why we deserve privacy and personal freedoms

a sunday in June 2026

I have tried to convince myself that they cannot steal my data if I just give it to them.

that there would be no attempts to bypass security walls if no walls exist in the first place.

now, I don’t find myself corrected. simply exposed to the train of thought i tried aggressively burying many years ago as a teenager who, in her bid to feel undefeated, pretended to not care.

fast forward to today when i am reminded how personal liberties like privacy aren’t as simply defined as an individual empowered with the ability to determine the outcomes of one’s life. It is faith in the nature of humanity itself.

many might not agree with many of the thoughts i have about human nature but it would take little effort to convince the intended:

we are deeply flawed.

you are reminded of it when you hear the voice in your head screaming obscenities or siding with the irrational. you are forced to bow to the acknowledgement of the inherent human imperfection within you each time you have to fight the urge to unwillingly hate or binge or be greedy and envious or violent.

man's first sin was disobedience to God. man’s second sin was considering himself to be God. he considered himself strong and superior and smart, not understanding this was power vested in him by god. why then do you and i, imperfect mortals, deserve the liberty to make our decisions and shape the trajectory of ours, and all the lives we come to interact with, in the process?

because two things can be right at the same time and we are flawed but gifted. we are evil and kind. greedy and giving. compassionate and envious. but most importantly, we can think. and when one thinks, he will know: himself, and the consequences of his actions, as well as basic math, and hopefully, game theory.

which screams that kindness is not a moral ideal but a mathematically superior survival strategy in repeated interactions. It demonstrates that mutual cooperation, and not ruthless selfishness, generates the highest long-term value for everyone involved.
