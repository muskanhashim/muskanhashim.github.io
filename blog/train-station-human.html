the man at the train station

august 15 2025

i see a alcoholic homeless human, who happens to be addicted to alcohol, at the train station every day. this morning he had a one litre flask of beer; he started drinking early today. he really seemed like the happiest man on earth, sitting in a pool of his own urine on the ground outside the train station.

he started at 9 am because someone, instead of buying him water, gave him enough money to buy his beer early on. yesterday he seemed sad and angry, almost agitated, and frustrated. he couldn't give in to his addiction because he didn't have enough money to pour into a substance that slowly destroys his body and organs from the inside.

it made me realize that sometimes we pray for things desperately, with the might of all our heart, believing that one thing will finally make us happy. because we don't know god's plan, we become agitated when we don't receive it, without realizing that not getting it may be his ultimate protection.

note: if you see a homeless human who happens to be addicted to alcohol, try talking to them. or just offer the shortest smile. alcohol dehydrates you; buy them water. take that sandwich out of your bag. they need it more than you do. that one meal could be the buffer their stomach is begging for just to stay alive.
