dear german dad,

written may 31 (and this is significant), 2026

all great stories start with someone asking a stranger for a light.

and so did you. little did i know, helping you out with your cigarette would mean you’d take a silent vow to not let drunk and lost (in more ways than one) me make any stupid decisions that night, such as crashing strangers’ parties at 2 am.

but you did. and then you refused to leave my side until i was home. which was complicated because home was a little far and so we walked. you lived on top of a hill (at that time), yet you walked miles in the opposite direction to make sure i was where i should have been all along, in my room, and safe.

and it’s weird how a drunk meeting at 2 am somehow turned into a 7 hour long conversation, with a goodbye at 9 am, after gas station coffee and sandwiches by the neckar.

in the coming weeks you reminded me of the reason for living. and for more than one week, it was only our weekend plans that kept me going through the week.

i’m sorry about your mom. and i know you don’t hold it against your dad, but he didn’t deserve you: just like mine didn’t.

thank you for taking me to the zoos. i’ll always cherish that. thank you for giving me a phone without me asking because i kept breaking mine. thank you for giving me your favorite jacket (you really loved it, you said it was exactly like vincent kompany’s).

i never told you this but for the short bit you were my dad, thanks for being a good dad.

i’ve moved away but you’d be proud of me.

stop smiling. stop always making fun of me. for some reason, whenever i think of you, i think of your insufferable unerasable little grin.

i’m sorry i was so bad at staying in touch.
